Flowing through dense old-growth forests of Douglas fir and Port Orford cedar, southern Oregon's Elk River is home to one of the finest fisheries on the West Coast. Each year, healthy runs of coho, fall Chinook, winter steelhead and sea-run cutthroat trout return to the Elk's shady emerald waters to spawn. Coho densities on the Elk are some of the highest in all of Oregon, making this a stream of utmost importance to the survival of this imperiled species. Oregon coho are listed as Threatened on the Endangered Species Act.

In 2012, Western Rivers Conservancy successfully conveyed a 170-acre property on the Elk to the Rogue River-Siskiyou National Forest. Protection of this property, which lies at the confluence of the Elk River and Rock Creek, forever conserves some of the most important coho spawning habitat in the entire Elk system.

The Elk River basin is also home to a wealth of other wildlife, including black-tailed deer, elk, black bear, mountain lion, red-legged frog, northern river otter, bald eagle and marbled murrelet, a rare and imperiled seabird which nests in old-growth forest along the West Coast.
